Based on the provided manuscript guidelines for the Damascus University Journal, here are the extracted formatting and submission rules:

### **1. General Requirements**

- The manuscript must not exceed 25 pages.
    
- The abstract must be 300 words or less.
    
- Two abstracts are required: one in Arabic and a second one in English.
    

### **2. Author Information**

- The document must include the authors' first names, surnames, scientific titles, research centers/universities, specific specializations, and university email addresses.
    
- The corresponding author must be marked with an asterisk (*) above their name, regardless of author order.
    

### **3. Page Setup & Layout**

- **Margins:** The top margin must be 3 cm. The bottom, left, and right margins must be 2 cm.
    
- **Headers & Footers:** Headers should be 1.8 cm from the top, and footers should be 0.6 cm from the bottom. The document should be set to have different odd and even pages.
    
- **Line Numbering:** Line numbers should be placed on the left side for articles written in Arabic. For articles written in English, line numbers should be on the right side.
    

### **4. Typography & Spacing**

- **Arabic Text:** Must use the "Simplified Arabic" font at size 12.
    
- **English/Latin Text:** Must use the "Times New Roman" font at size 11.
    
- **Titles:** Main titles must be size 16 and bold. Subtitles must be size 14 and bold.
    
- **Spacing:** Line spacing must be set to "Single". No extra spacing should be added between paragraphs of the same style.
    

### **5. Article Structure**

The manuscript must be organized in the following order:

1. **Introduction:** Introduces the topic and objectives.
    
2. **Literature Review:** Discusses previous studies, comparing similarities, differences, methodologies, and relevant results.
    
3. **Materials and Methods:** Details the study population, sample, and data collection tools.
    
4. **Results and Discussion**.
    
5. **Conclusions:** Briefly stated in numbered paragraphs.
    
6. **References:** Placed at the very end of the article.
    

### **6. Tables and Figures**

- **Tables:** Must be numbered sequentially according to their appearance. The title goes _above_ the table in bold, size 10 font. Any references, notes, or symbol explanations go _below_ the table in regular, size 10 font.
    
- **Figures:** Must be numbered sequentially. The title, along with any symbol explanations or references, goes _below_ the figure in bold, size 10 font.
    

### **7. Citations and References**

- **Reference List Order:** The list must begin with Arabic references, followed by English references. Entries must be sorted alphabetically (A to Z).
    
- **Formatting Titles:** Titles of books, theses, conference papers, and journals must be italicized instead of using bold or underlined text.
    
- **DOI:** If a reference has a DOI number, it must be included at the end of the citation.
    
- **In-Text Citations:** Citations in the text generally include the author's surname, year, and page number, either narratively or in parentheses at the end of the sentence.
    
- **Multiple Authors (In-Text):** For works with multiple authors, cite the first author's surname followed by "et al." (or "وأخرون" in Arabic) .
    
- **Multiple Sources (In-Text):** When citing multiple references at once, separate them with a semicolon and list them in alphabetical order.
